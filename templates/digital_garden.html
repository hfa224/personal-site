What is a digital garden?

A garden is a collection of evolving ideas that aren't strictly organised by their publication date. They're inherently exploratory â€“ notes are linked through contextual associations. They aren't refined or complete - notes are published as half-finished thoughts that will grow and evolve over time. They're less rigid, less performative, and less perfect than the personal websites we're used to seeing.

My digital gardens

Kinopio is the space I think of as my digital garden - I love the mind mapping aspect of it and I can't think of anything else on the internet like it. Some people find it chaotic, but the way you can "pin" things in a space and link them up is the most intuitive to my brain, and the aesthetic helps a lot too (if things look nice, I am more likely to use them).

As the developer says in this blog post, Kinopio is designed to "Get the chaotic messy thoughts and ideas out of your head" and it does that for me!
