What is a digital garden?

A garden is a collection of evolving ideas that aren't strictly organised by their publication date. They're inherently exploratory â€“ notes are linked through contextual associations. They aren't refined or complete - notes are published as half-finished thoughts that will grow and evolve over time. They're less rigid, less performative, and less perfect than the personal websites we're used to seeing.

My digital gardens

Kinopio is the space I think of as my digital garden - I love the mind mapping aspect of it and I can't think of anything else on the internet like it. Some people find it chaotic, but the way you can "pin" things in a space and link them up is the most intuitive to my brain, and the aesthetic helps a lot too (if things look nice, I am more likely to use them).

As the developer says in this blog post, Kinopio is designed to "Get the chaotic messy thoughts and ideas out of your head" and it does that for me!

Other small/indie web sites

What is the small web? See Small is Beautiful, but for me it's also an aesthetic thing. Maybe indie web is a better term.

In addition to Kinopio I've been trying out a lot of "small web" ajacent sites and service recently - here are a couple.

  • omg.lol
    • This links to my omg.lol page - I don't use it much at the moment, but it's fun and interesting as a web portal. I originally signed up to use their picture hosting for my photos, but it wasn't really what I wanted - which is why I created this site!
  • Bear blog
    • (this is really under construction!) This links to my bear blog, which is really under construction - I'm also not sure if I'll share it here long term, as I've been using it for quite personal blogging where there's freedom in anonimity.
  • Yay boo
    • This is a site that will let you upload very small static sites. Could be quite fun and useful if you're messing around with like, a pgae of html.
  • python anywhere
    • I used this to host the initial iteration of the book club site I made - it was very easy to deploy a flask app here! Ultimately I decided to host it as a static site (because I felt it didn't really need any interaction elements)
